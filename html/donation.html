Donation Types & FAQs

Whole Blood

Most common donation. Used in surgery and trauma cases.

Platelets

Used by cancer patients and those with clotting problems.

Plasma

Used for patients with liver failure and other conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)'s

Does donating blood hurt?

Most donors feel only a brief pinch when the needle is inserted. The discomfort is minimal and lasts only a few seconds.

How long does the donation process take?

The entire process usually takes 45–60 minutes. The blood donation itself takes about 10 minutes.

Is donating blood safe?

Yes. All equipment used is sterile and single-use.Donations follow strict medical safety standards.

Can I donate if I am taking medication?

Some medications may affect eligibility. Please inform staff of any medications before donating.

How often can I donate blood?

Whole blood can be donated every 56 days, platelets every 7 days, and plasma every 28 days.

What should I do before donating blood?

Eat a healthy meal, drink fluids, rest well, avoid alcohol for 24 hours, and bring a valid ID.

What should I do after donating blood?

Rest briefly, drink fluids, avoid heavy lifting, and keep the bandage on for several hours.

Who receives my donated blood?

Donated blood helps accident victims, surgical patients, cancer patients, newborns, and those with chronic illnesses.

Can I donate if I have a tattoo or piercing?

In most cases, donation is allowed 6–12 months after a tattoo or piercing, depending on regulations.

What if I am afraid of needles?

Staff are trained to support nervous donors and will guide you through the process calmly and professionally.

What are high-risk countries for blood donation?

High-risk countries are regions where infections such as malaria are common. Travel to parts of Africa, Asia and South America may result in a temporary deferral from donating blood.

Deferral periods usually range from 3 to 12 months, depending on the countryvisited and the length of stay. Eligibility rules vary, so donors shouldcheck with their local blood bank.
